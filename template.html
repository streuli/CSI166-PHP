Weather cycles refer to the natural variations in weather patterns that occur over different periods. These cycles can range from daily changes to long-term patterns like seasons. Some key weather cycles include the diurnal cycle, which involves daily changes in temperature and humidity, and the seasonal cycle, where weather patterns shift due to the tilt of the Earth's axis as it orbits the sun. Longer-term cycles include phenomena like El Niño and La Niña, which are part of the El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO) and can significantly impact global weather patterns. Additionally, there are multi-decadal cycles like the Atlantic Multidecadal Oscillation (AMO) and the Pacific Decadal Oscillation (PDO), which influence climate patterns over periods of several decades. These cycles are driven by various factors, including changes in solar radiation, ocean currents, and atmospheric circulation. They all play a crucial role in shaping the weather we experience on a daily basis.
